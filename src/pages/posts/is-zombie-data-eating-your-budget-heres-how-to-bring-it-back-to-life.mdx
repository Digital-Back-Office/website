### What Exactly Is Zombie Data?


Zombie Data refers to data assets that are siloed, outdated, or disconnected from your active business intelligence tools. It has "no brain" — disconnected from the central systems that drive decisions. It cannot contribute to customers, analytics, or predictions.

Usually it appears in three forms:

- **Legacy Systems:** Old CRMs or ERPs that were "retired" but never fully migrated.
- **Shadow IT:** Spreadsheets and local databases created by teams that don’t talk to central IT.
- **Unstructured Backups:** Massive dumps of emails, PDFs, or logs in cold storage that were never indexed.

### The 3 Risks of the "Undead" Data Approach

Leaving these files isn't just housekeeping; it's risk. Here’s how Zombie Data threatens your business:

1. **The Financial Drain**

   Storage is cheap, but it isn't free. Storing terabytes of Redundant, Obsolete, or Trivial (ROT) data adds up fast — you're paying rent for a tenant who never pays you back.

2. **The Security Liability**

   Attackers love forgotten data. Zombie Data is often less protected yet may contain sensitive PII or IP. If you don't know it exists, you can't secure it or comply with GDPR/CCPA.

3. **The AI "Garbage In" Problem**

   Feed a model a dataset clogged with Zombie Data, duplicates, and old formats, and the results will be poor. High-performing AI needs clean, relevant, and active data.
